# Los-Angeles-crime-analysis
Crime pattern analysis in Los Angeles using Python (EDA + Geospatial + Temporal Analysis)
#  Los Angeles Crime Analysis Project (2020 - 2024)

##  Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of crime patterns in Los Angeles from 2020 to 2024. By analyzing over 800,000 crime records, the study aims to identify temporal trends, high-risk locations, and victim demographics to support data-driven public safety strategies.

---

##  Key Insights & Findings

### 1. Temporal Patterns (The "When")
* **Peak Hours:** Crime incidents significantly surge during the **Night** and **Afternoon** periods.
* **Reporting Spikes:** A massive reporting peak occurs at **12:00 PM**, likely due to crimes discovered during midday breaks.
* **Monthly Trends:** **January** consistently records the highest volume of incidents, followed by a slight decline towards the end of the year.

### 2. Geospatial Analysis (The "Where")
* **Dangerous Hubs:** **Central** and **77th Street** areas are identified as the most critical hotspots, especially during nighttime.
* **Crime Specialization:** * **77th Street** is a primary target for **Vehicle Theft**.
    * **Central Area** suffers heavily from **Burglary from Vehicles** due to its commercial nature.
* **Primary Locations:** The **Street** is the #1 location for crime, followed closely by **Single-Family Dwellings** and **Apartments**.

### 3. Victim & Weapon Analysis (The "Who & How")
* **Demographics:** Males and the **Hispanic (H)** community are disproportionately affected by criminal activity.
* **Age Factor:** Most victims are aged between **20 and 40**.
* **Modus Operandi:** While most crimes involve **No Weapon**, **"Strong-Arm"** (physical force) is the most common method in violent encounters.

### 4. Legal Status
* **Efficiency Gap:** Approximately **80% of cases** remain under **"Investigation Continued" (Invest Cont)**, highlighting a significant backlog or challenge in clearing property-related crimes.
